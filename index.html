Re-learning

Too many classes


Many struggling high school students believe that there are too many classes. It's a common phrase used to excuse for your academic struggles. However, as stereotypical as it is, it does hold some meaning. I, myself, am a high school student and occasionally, this common phrase does apply. Though I only have 6 classes, it sometimes feels as if there were too many classes to record. The caliber of homework and tests only emphasize how 6 classes sometimes equate to a greater number of less-strenuous classes. This very idea contributes to our negligence of class material because our days pass by quickly and the constant rush of going from class to class sometimes overrule your thought process or your comprehension of class material.

Difficulty comprehending


We travel from class to class on a daily basis. We constantly find ourselves either bored or unattentive. Either this or a few rare exceptions where the teacher is not providing their fair share of help. Many high school students like myself run into issues where they leave a class completely forgetting the meaning of the lecture or the reason for completing an in-class assignment. We tend to forget the information we so-called "learned" from class the very next day because we don't constantly exercise it. Though it is important to be attentive in class, there might not always be an equal opportunity for one to learn. Whether it is the occasion or the environment you surround yourself with, you might find it difficult grasping the true meaning behind the teacher's words and lessons.

Difficult class


And of course, we cannot forget about the unrelenting difficulty of our classes. Whether they require you to self-study or they require you to put in extra time, they will, regardless of your intelligence, require you to put in some sort of effort. The difficulty of the class combined with the strain of having multiple work-intensive classes and your ability to comprehend class material can potentially add up to an endless loop where you are constantly tailing your classes and spending extra time to understand the information. Difficult classes only emphasize the need to comprehend the information. They encourage us to seek an outer source for help and comfort knowing that tomorrow's test will not be a complete failure. Introduction
Hey, my name is Vincent Tu. You might be asking yourself why I chose these photos. Well, I chose these pictures because they represent an aspect of me. I love to swim and I enjoy spending time with friends and volunteering. I'm currently in my junior year in high school and I am tackling a few difficult AP classes. I noticed that I, like many people, tend to forget what they learn in class and this is detrimental to saving time and ultimately saving yourself hours of pointless studying. Moving on, this website's purpose is to provide a platform for you to review over course material. Now I understand that this is based on my classes and that this website is still in it's infant stage, but I welcome people to contribute to it. I created this website to house the majority of the information I learn in all my classes. It was not only made to help myself with my own tedious studies but it is also made to aid other people in my class who seek help. I know I might not be the most active and avid volunteer but that doesn't stop me from doing something that I enjoy as a hobby while helping others.


A Little More About Me
Another hobby of mine is coding. I became interested in computers around 7th grade when I took an introductory course into computer hardware. In the following school year, I took Photoshop and Animation to experience the graphic designing world. Towards 9th grade, I began to learn an introductory language called QBASIC. After 9th grade, I was constantly encumbered by school work which meant that I couldn't spend much time learning about such things. Here in 11th grade, I am currently taking Java which included an introduction to Python. Recently (beginning in the summer of 11th grade), I learned Python by myself along with a bit of NumPy, matplotlib, and Pandas which are all data manipulation/analysis modules of Python. When my junior year began, I took interest to learning a bit of web development. I learned HTML, CSS, and a bit of Javascript, Bootstrap, and scss. This website is an example of my work. I'm planning on making many more things with coding but that will have to wait because of school!


Addressing the Initial Question: Why?
I also understand why someone would question why I would make this website when there are plenty of other websites to help people with their classes. To that question, I say that this website is just a fun, beneficial project for me which has the option to help you if you need it. I'd like to experiment more with new things like web development and despite discouragements as to why I would make a website that serves nearly no purpose to some, I would, regardless of this, still attempt to do it because it's a learning and growing experience.

Java: Java class features an introductory course with Python which goes over concepts prevalent among computer languages. It also, as the name implies, features a great deal of Java-related projects. I will be mainly explaining the Python and Java projects.

AP Calculus AB: AP Calculus AB features Calculus of course, but also many intracies. Over this course, I will be trying my best to cover pre-established topics as well as topics that we have covered in class. Besides the calculus, the class is also heavily algebra based and requires a strong foundation in algebra and trigonometry as there are a great deal of formulas to memorize.

AP Chinese: AP Chinese like any other AP course is a work-intensive class that is fast paced. Over this course, I will be trying to cover the topics we covered in class and also the words we learned from these lessons.

AP U.S. History: This class is not only strenuous, but it is also heavily leveraged on your ability to remember and recall many certain events or time periods. These complexities require students to dedicate a great deal of time to either studying or comprehending the information. For this class, I will be trying to cover specific time periods along with a number of important events.

AP Language: AP Language is one of the most critical-thinking-based classes to my knowledge. A variety of essays combined with in-depth reading comprehension and literary analysis topics render this class difficult. For this class, I will be mainly managing and showcasing recommended words-to-know, essay structures, literary terms, and strategies.

AP Statistics: AP Statistics, at least for me personally, I believe is the most thought provoking class. The abstract concepts and odd relationships between mathematically-sound concepts really expand the horizon simple mathematical concepts. I will be explaining certain concepts as to help myself and possibly you clarify certain misconceptions.
